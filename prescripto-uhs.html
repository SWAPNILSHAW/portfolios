Introduction

Prescripto is a modern healthcare platform prototype that addresses fragmentation across patient-facing services, clinical workflows, emergency response, and insurance processing. The platform concentrates on improving care continuity, reducing duplicate diagnostics, and enabling faster emergency coordination.

Problem Statement (High-level)

  • Disjointed patient experiences across many apps and providers.
  • Scattered medical records leading to repeated tests and care gaps.
  • Slow, manual emergency coordination and blood availability checks.
  • Cumbersome, paper-heavy insurance claim flows.

Solution Overview (Redacted)

Prescripto provides a unified interface for patients and a consolidated backend for hospitals and partners. Key capabilities include appointment booking, unified medical records (consent-based), medicine ordering, ambulance dispatching, emergency blood coordination, and insurance pre-authorization.

Note: Detailed architecture diagrams, partner contract specifics, deployment topology, exact algorithms, and financial terms are not published here and are treated as confidential.

Core Features (Summarized)

  • Unified access for patients to clinical and non-clinical services.
  • Cross-provider record access controlled by patient consent.
  • Real-time emergency workflows for ambulances and blood requests.
  • AI-assisted triage and preventive insights (model details withheld).
  • Insurance integration for pre-authorizations and streamlined claims.

Architected for scalability and security. Sensitive implementation specifics (e.g., exact cloud topology, internal endpoints, keys, and partner integrations) are redacted. The system enforces role-based access control, encryption in transit and at rest, and audit logging for compliance.
